01 · DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Four commitments that shape every screen.

The previous spec tried to solve everything. This one solves the one question we ask most: where are we vs. budget, and what's coming? Everything else is in service of that.

01
One screen does the heavy lifting.
The Dashboard answers the daily questions. Other tabs are for editing, not for checking. If we open the app and immediately need to click somewhere, the design has failed.
02
Charts on top, details below.
Every screen has a visual at the top and a clickable detail panel underneath. Click a budget bar, the panel below changes. Click a vendor, the panel changes. Predictable everywhere.
03
Email proposes. We dispose.
Parsed receipts and estimates always require one click of confirmation. Nothing silently mutates the budget. Manual entry is always an option for cash, family checks, paper receipts.
04
Payer is a first-class field.
Every dollar belongs to someone: us, Atharva's parents, Celesia's mom. Color-coded everywhere so we can scan the cash flow and know whose check is next.
02 · VISUAL SYSTEM

Editorial, not SaaS dashboard.

This is something we will look at twice a day for 19 months. It should feel like a magazine we want to open, not a Jira board we have to. Warm cream surface, deep ink, terracotta accent that quietly nods to the cultural blend, restrained use of color elsewhere.

Persistent, never hidden.

B · KPI STRIP
Four numbers. No more.
Planned · Committed · Paid · Health. Health is qualitative ("On track" / "Slipping" / "At risk") so we feel the state, not just count it.

Always visible.
Every payment line shows who's paying with a small chip. Atharva's parents · Celesia's mom · Us. We can scan the cash flow and know whose check is next.

A · ONE BAR
The whole budget at a glance.
Eight color-coded segments, sized to amount. F&B is the giant; Invites is barely there. Edited segments get a gold outline so you can see what shifted.
B · VARIANCE TRACK
Drift, made visible.
Center tick = locked baseline. Fills left (under) or right (over) as you adjust. Tolerance band ±5% — cross it and the bar turns red.
C · EDIT INLINE
No save button.
Click any planned number. Type. Tab out. The bar, the variance, the payer split — all update. Edits sync to Postgres immediately, mirror to the Sheet within five minutes.
D · EDITED ROWS
What did I just change?
Gold border on inputs that differ from baseline. Variance column shows +$ or –$. Revert button at the top resets everything in one click.
E · PAYER PICKER
Click any chip · reassign in two taps.
Every payer chip is a button. Click it, pick a new payer from the three options (or add a new one). The category's default payer changes, future payments inherit the new default, and the panel below recalculates. Specific contracts can still override.
F · LIVE PAYER PANEL
Before you call your mom.
Shift a category to a different payer, the totals shift instantly. See exactly what you're asking from each family before sending an email about it.
G · SCENARIOS
Try without committing.
Save the current state as a named alternative ("Drop videographer", "Add Sunday brunch"). Switch between them. Only "Lock as baseline" overwrites the real budget.

A · THREE COLUMNS
Time, not status.
We organize by when, not what. Behind = past due. Now = next 14 days. Upcoming = 15-60 days. Beyond 60 days lives in the list view.
B · DEPENDENCY HINT
"Blocks" and "Blocked by".
Each card surfaces its dependency relationships in plain English. Red text when something we need is late. We can spot a chain reaction before it happens.
C · OWNER CHIP
Who has the ball.
Single owner is preferred. "Both" is allowed but discouraged. The chip uses the same color system as payers so we can scan ownership across the app.
D · OTHER VIEWS
List, calendar, graph.
Board is the default. List is for bulk edits. Calendar is for the week-of view late in planning. Dependency graph is the "why is this stuck?" view.

A · INTENT
Five buckets, color-coded.
Receipt · Invoice · Estimate · Contract · Info. Confidence label tells us how sure the parser is. We always see this before deciding.
B · SUGGESTION
One sentence + three buttons.
"Looks like a receipt for $X. Match against Y?" Confirm, Edit, or Ignore. Never silent. Never destructive.
C · MATCHING
98% match earns confidence.
Receipts that cleanly match a scheduled payment by amount + vendor + date show high confidence. Anything ambiguous lands in "Edit" mode by default.
D · NO REPLY UI
We never send from here.
Per safety rules, the tool reads from the dedicated inbox but never replies. Replies happen in Gmail. The "create task" action just files a reminder.
09 · DATA ARCHITECTURE

Live data + a spreadsheet shadow.

The app needs to be fast for us, auditable for everyone, and editable in a fallback when the app is down or unfamiliar to whoever's looking. The right answer: Postgres as the source of truth, auto-mirrored to a Google Sheet every five minutes. The app stays snappy. The Sheet is a live read-only window — family can open it, Atharva can build pivot tables on top of it, and if anything ever breaks, the Sheet is the backup that rebuilds the database.

Three architectures considered

OPTION A
Google Sheet is the database
App ↔ Sheets API
(read/write on every request)
Auditable by design · revision history is free
Family edits directly · no separate UI to learn
Zero infrastructure to deploy
Slow · 200–500ms per call · cold-start pages take 2–3s
Rate limits matter for batch operations
No transactions · concurrent edits clash
No proper foreign keys · referential integrity is on you
OPTION B
Postgres + nightly export to Sheet
App ↔ Postgres
Postgres → nightly cron → Sheet
Fast app · proper relational integrity
Sheet still exists as a backup
Sheet is stale for up to 24 hours
Not "live" in the way you asked for
Two systems · two places to look
RECOMMENDED
OPTION C
Postgres + 5-min mirror to Sheet
App ↔ Postgres (real-time)
Postgres → 5-min cron → Sheet
App is snappy · sub-100ms reads
Sheet is live-enough (5-min lag is invisible)
Sheet revision history = free audit trail
Family gets a familiar interface to look at
Sheet snapshot becomes a true backup
Slightly more setup · one cron job to maintain
Alternative worth knowing about Airtable could replace both Postgres and the Sheet. It's a spreadsheet-shaped database with native record history, sharable views, and a generous API. The 1,000-record free tier covers this wedding easily. The reason it's not the recommendation: Vercel + Postgres is a tighter native integration and gives you full SQL when you want it, which on a data-flavored project you probably will.

How the pieces fit together

Live in-app, always.
Edits go straight to Postgres. Other browser tabs revalidate every 30 seconds via SWR. For two-to-three people, that's "live enough."
Auditable, three ways.
(1) activity_log table records every change with user + timestamp + diff. (2) Google Sheet revision history. (3) Sheet is a snapshot you can always roll back from.
Family-visible.
Share the Sheet read-only with anyone. They see vendors, contracts, payments, tasks — one tab per table — without needing app accounts.
A note on access You said "anyone for now." A Vercel-hosted app with no auth means anyone who finds the URL sees the full budget and every payer detail. Even obscure URLs leak (a screenshot, a forwarded link). Recommend the lightest possible auth: magic-link via Resend with an email allowlist. ~30 lines of code, no passwords to manage, you control who has access by editing a list. Family gets in by entering their email; the Sheet stays the share-with-everyone surface. This is the kind of safety net that costs nothing today and prevents one bad day eighteen months from now.
10 · DECISIONS

All six questions, answered.

Locked in. Claude Code can treat these as constraints, not options.

01
Hosted web app on Vercel. Next.js (App Router) + TypeScript · Vercel hosting · Vercel Postgres · Vercel Cron for scheduled jobs. Native, free-tier-friendly stack, one vendor for the whole loop.
02
Dedicated Gmail: celesia.atharva@gmail.com Gmail OAuth2, read-only scope, polled every 5 minutes via Vercel Cron. The app never sends from this address — replies happen in Gmail directly.
03
Open access for now · with magic-link allowlist suggested. No role-based permissions. Everyone who's signed in can see and edit everything. The Sheet mirror is the share-with-anyone surface.
04
Desktop-first. Layouts above are sized for 1280px+ screens. Mobile works but is not optimized. Phone is for glancing, laptop is for editing.
05
Rules + regex parsing in V1. Heuristics for amount extraction, sender-domain matching against vendors, intent classification by subject keywords. LLM-assisted parsing is a V2 upgrade if accuracy is poor — gate it on actual evidence.
06
Budget locked at $142,682 · editable via the Budget Editor. The locked baseline is the No Brunch / Mid Dec scenario. Edits are tracked as variance from baseline. "Save as scenario" lets us try alternatives without losing the locked number.
